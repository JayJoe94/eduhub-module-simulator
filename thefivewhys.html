Module 5: Business Scenarios & Applications for the 5 Whys Technique

1) Why this matters Warm‑up

Data tells you what happened. The 5 Whys helps you get to why it happened, so your actions hit the root cause, not the symptom.

Reflection prompt

Think of a recent issue in your work. What was the surface symptom? What might be a deeper cause?

2) Healthcare — Reduce ER Wait Times

Scenario: Long ER waits. Goal: < 1 hour.

Why 1: Why are patients waiting so long?

Not enough clinicians scheduled during peak hours (2–6 pm).

Why 2: Why not enough clinicians at peaks?

Schedules don’t match demand patterns.

Why 3: Why don’t schedules match demand?

Staffing plan relies on outdated arrival data.

Why 4: Why is the data outdated?

No real‑time patient flow tracking.

Why 5: Why no real‑time tracking?

Budget not allocated for the upgrade.

Action: Add real‑time tracking; re‑optimize rosters to peak demand.
Check your thinking

What additional metrics would you monitor (e.g., triage-to-room time, LWBS rate, bed turnover)?

3) Finance — Drop in Credit Card Usage

Scenario: 15% Q3 usage drop, concentrated ages 25–34.

Why 1: Why did usage drop?

Customers moved spend to a competitor card.

Why 2: Why switch to competitor?

More compelling online‑shopping rewards.

Why 3: Why were their rewards better?

They tailored benefits after segment research.

Why 4: Why didn’t we match?

No segment‑specific analysis or testing.

Why 5: Why no targeted analysis?

Data tools aren’t optimized for cohort insights.

Action: Launch targeted rewards for 25–34; run rapid A/B tests; instrument cohort analytics.
Mini‑challenge

List datasets to review before launch (e.g., wallet‑share, merchant mix, churn risk, CAC/LTV by cohort).

4) Marketing — High Engagement, Low Conversions

Scenario: Instagram ads: CTR 8% (great) → conversion 0.5% (poor).

Why 1: Why low sales despite high engagement?

Users drop after clicking.

Why 2: Why the post‑click drop?

Mobile site loads slowly.

Why 3: Why slow on mobile?

Images not optimized; heavy scripts.

Why 4: Why not optimized?

No process/ownership for mobile performance.

Why 5: Why no process?

No scheduled audits or SLAs.

Action: Optimize images & scripts; add quarterly performance audits; test checkout UX.
Quick poll (discuss)

Would speed fixes be enough, or would you also test offer, product‑page layout, trust badges, and payment options?

Tips

  • Use data to validate each “Why”.
  • Stop when the answer becomes a process/policy you can change.
  • Branch if multiple plausible causes appear.
  • Capture actions tied to the root cause.

6) Key Takeaways

  • Data analysis explains what; 5 Whys uncovers why.
  • Works across healthcare, finance, marketing—and beyond.
  • Design actions that address root causes, not symptoms.
